Healing Properties of Fruits

Consuming a variety of fruits is great for your health as it offers the body essential nutrients and antioxidants that improve our overall wellbeing and reduce the chances of illnesses. Fruits contain significant quantities of fiber and they contain essential minerals and vitamins. Additionally, fruits contain antioxidants like flavonoids that improve overall wellbeing. A diet rich in fruits and vegetable can lower the likelihood of developing heart diseases, inflammation, cancer and diabetes.

  • Apples

    • Apples have a wide range of delicious varieties so its easy to find one that suits your taste. Apples have tremendous health benefits as they regulate blood sugar levels, enhance brain function, strengthen the immune system, lower bad cholesterol, and improve memory. Apples are an excellent source of fiber, which promotes weight loss and keeps the heart in good condition.
    To obtain the maximum health benefits, it is recommended to eat apples with the skin on as it contain significant amounts of calcium. The pectin found in apples promotes good gut health, and binds stool, making them beneficial in cases of diarrhea. The quercetin found in apples possesses anti-cancer properties. Overall, apples have so much to offer to us and we should try to incorporate some apples into our daily meals.

  • Oranges

    • Oranges are abundant in essential vitamins and minerals. With one medium sized orange providing 117% of the recommended daily intake of vitamin c, it is regarded as one of the best natural sources of this vitamin which is a very potent antioxidant that strengthens immune functions and promotes absorption of iron from plant-based foods in the body. The vitamin C in oranges is very instrumental in preventing DNA mutation and development of cancerous cells. The peel of oranges is known for its carminative and stimulant properties, and it has been used to cure various ailments like bronchitis, cough, diabetes and liver disorders. Oranges are rich in pectin, which promotes colon health by fusing with harmful chemicals and obliterating them from the colon. Oranges also contain B-vitamins like thiamin and folate which maintain healthy nervous and reproductive systems and promote red blood cells production

  • Watermelon

    • Watermelons are very refreshing and contain about 90% water which makes them an ideal option to keep the body hydrated. The high antioxidant content in watermelon particularly vitamin c, promotes collagen production in the body resulting in healthier skin and offers protection against asthma. Additionally, watermelon contains as L-citrulline and L-arginine, two essential antioxidants that can reduce blood pressure and improve artery function. Watermelons also promote digestion and improve gut health due to their high fiber content. Being 90% water, it is rich in electrolytes such as potassium, and promotes hydration of the body. Watermelon is very useful in eliminating excess fluids from the body which can be beneficial for people with kidney problems and high blood pressure. Watermelon can also reduce muscle soreness and hasten recovery time after exercise.

  • Papaya

    • Papaya is a great fruit. It takes the semblance of a pear but when it Is fully ripe its skin is orange. Papaya is widely known for its tremendous curative qualities. Modern urban lifestyles, characterized by hurried mealtimes, can place a great burden on the digestive system, leading many individuals to resort to digestive aids in the form of medication. However, papaya is an excellent source of digestive enzymes that facilitate the breakdown of food and proves to be a more healthy approach when incorporated into our diet. Papaya also improves bowel movements, and gets rid of intestinal parasites. Those suffering from reflux, ulcers and heartburn often find relief in consuming papaya regularly. Furthermore, rubbing the inner skin of the fruit on the skin can alleviate eczema and dermatitis.
